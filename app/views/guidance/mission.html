Your mission is the thing you’re currently doing, what you want to achieve and who it is for.

Your mission statement describes your mission. It is a clear, meaningful and motivational statement that can be used as a guiding policy for decision making - does this work contribute to the mission?

There can be multiple missions that multiple teams are working on simultaneously.

A good mission statement should describe:

  • what do we do now?
  • who are we doing it for?
  • what are we trying to accomplish to solve the problem?
  • what impact are we trying to achieve?

Your mission should link to your strategy and vision. You should have a mission statement for each phase.

You should involve your team in setting your mission, which you can do by running a workshop.

You should share your mission statement regularly – such as in show and tells and meetings with stakeholders.

Writing your mission statement

Your mission statement should be written clearly and concisely. You should avoid acronyms and specialist language. A content designer can help with this.

You should test drafts of your mission statement with your users, team and stakeholders, to make sure they understand and agree.

Example mission statement

The example mission statement for the private beta phase of the imaginary service Get pencils for schools is:

We are building a digital service that enables school business professionals to easily order free pencils.
