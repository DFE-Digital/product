A vision describes what the world would look like if your digital service succeeds. It is a clear, concise and inspirational description of the desired long-term change resulting from your digital service, or programme's work. It should be aspirational, but achievable.

Your vision sets a North Star to give you and your teams direction. Your vision should describe what you want to achieve in the future in terms of an outcome, rather than a solution.

Your vision should link to your strategy and mission statement. You should use the same vision across all phases, with a specific strategy and mission statement for each phase.

You should involve your team in setting your vision, which you can do by running a workshop.

Writing your vision

Your vision should be written clearly and concisely. You should avoid acronyms and specialist language. A content designer can help with this.

You should test drafts of your vision with your users, team and stakeholders, to make sure they understand and agree.

Example vision

The example vision for the imaginary service Get pencils for schools is:

All school age children in England will have quick and easy access to as many pencils as they need.
