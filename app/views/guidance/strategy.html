A product strategy is the high-level plan of how you'll achieve your vision. A product strategy sits between your vision and roadmap. It describes how you intend to realise the vision, and provides the input to create a roadmap.

You should share your strategy regularly - such as in show and tells and meetings with stakeholders.

What to include in your strategy

Your strategy could include:

  • the policy and organisational context, and the problem you are trying to solve
  • he scope of your service - what it will do and what it will not do
  • who your users are, and who are not your users
  • high level user needs that you have identified through research
  • your high level delivery strategy - this may be your current roadmap, or may be a number of steps over multiple years
  • what success looks like, and how it will be measured
  • what risks might derail your plans, and how you will mitigate them

Writing your strategy

Your strategy should be written clearly and concisely. You should avoid acronyms and specialist language. It should be in an accessible format. A content designer can help with this.
