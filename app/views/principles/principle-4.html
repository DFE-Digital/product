Prioritisation: using evidence for strategic decision making

Why do we do this?

  • To deliver value and meet user needs as early as possible by prioritising value dense work
  • To ensure a shared understanding of the work that is required
  • To consider and balance risks and dependencies
  • Confirms that deliverables are worth the effort
  • To make sure our work aligns to organizational dependencies

How to approach it

  • Listen to users
  • Use all available evidence to understand the full view
  • Use an appropriate framework so you can justify the priority order
  • Engage all stakeholders to understand the value and aims
  • Ensure access to the relevant SMEs
  • Make data-driven decisions
  • Work towards the team being aligned in prioritisation decisions
  • The work required for sizing/estimation must be proportional to the task
  • Business benefit/value must be considered alongside user need​
  • Prioritisation can be collaborative, but ensure the Product Manager has full responsibility for final decisions.

Roadmapping

A roadmap is a prioritised sequence of outcomes that deliver the product strategy, realising value early, in the most effective way. It is part of your storytelling about what you’re doing and why.

Ideally it should be made up of measurable, outcome items, and be used to facilitate conversations with stakeholders.

It should

  • Use time horizons - Now (0-3 months), Next (3 to 6 months), Later (6 months+) with milestones only where necessary
  • Be outcome based (with an understanding of measurement)
  • Be clear and concise, written in plain English with minimal use of jargon and acronyms
  • Be In an accessible format
  • Stand on its own
  • Be reviewed monthly, updated at least quarterly
  • Be a collaborative document
  • Be owned by the Product Manager
  • Be relevant and accurate

What to avoid

  • Presenting it as an absolute (things change, it is reflective of a point in time)
  • Delivery dates (these should be on a delivery plan)
  • Creating a roadmap in isolation
  • A list of features - (features (the how) can change, the outcome (the what) rarely does)
  • Creating it in isolation - this is something that your team and your stakeholders should be aware of and bought in to.
